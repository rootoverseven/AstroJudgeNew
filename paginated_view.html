Vedic Analysis

In Jyotish, the Prakriti or natural temperament is determined by the elements of the Sun (Surya – The Soul), Moon (Chandra – The Mind), and Ascendant (Lagna – The Body). Here the Sun and Ascendant both sit in Scorpio (8), a Water (Jala) sign, while the Moon occupies Capricorn (10), an Earth (Prithvi) sign. Thus the triad is composed of two watery influences and one earthy influence, with no Fire (Agni) or Air (Vayu) component. This yields a Prakriti that is rich in emotional depth and practical grounding. The dominant Water element bestows intuition, sensitivity, and emotional intensity. The single Earth placement grounds these feelings with patience, stability, and a methodical approach. Without Fire, the native is less impulsively energetic or spotlight‑seeking, and without Air, the level of talkativeness or social agility is moderate. The soul’s core is therefore one that seeks deep connections, traditions, and a reliable sense of security, yet approaches change gradually and with contemplative care.
